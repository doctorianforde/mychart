## üíâ Logging Health Readings

### How to Log Blood Pressure

1. **Scroll to "Log Blood Pressure Reading" section**

2. **Fill in the form:**
   - **Date:** Select the date you took the reading
   - **Time of Reading:** Enter the time (or click "Now" for current time)
   - **Reading Site:** Choose where you measured (left arm, right arm, left wrist, right wrist)
   - **Systolic (Top Number):** Example: 120
   - **Diastolic (Bottom Number):** Example: 80
   - **Pulse (BPM):** Example: 70

3. **Click "Log BP Reading"**

4. **Reading appears in Medical Records** section below

#### Blood Pressure Guidelines

| Category | Systolic | Diastolic | Flag |
|----------|----------|-----------|------|
| **Low** | < 90 | OR < 60 | üî¥ DANGER |
| **Normal** | 90-139 | AND 60-89 | ‚úÖ Normal |
| **High** | 140-180 | OR 90-120 | ‚ö†Ô∏è High |
| **Hypertensive Crisis** | > 180 | OR > 120 | üî¥ DANGER |

#### Important Alerts

**DANGER Readings (Hypertensive Crisis or Low BP):**
- You'll see an alert dialog
- **An email is automatically sent to the office**
- **Consider contacting emergency services if you feel unwell**

**How to Measure Blood Pressure Correctly:**

‚úÖ **Be still** - No smoking, caffeine, or exercise 30 minutes before
‚úÖ **Sit correctly** - Back straight, feet flat on floor, legs uncrossed
‚úÖ **Bare arm** - Roll up your sleeve
‚úÖ **Take multiple readings** - At least two readings, one minute apart

See the diagram on the dashboard for proper posture.

### How to Log Glucose Reading

1. **Scroll to "Log Glucose Reading" section**

2. **Fill in the form:**
   - **Date:** Select the date you took the reading
   - **Time of Reading:** Enter when you tested (or click "Now")
   - **Glucose (mg/dL):** Enter your blood sugar level (example: 120)
   - **Time of Last Meal:** When did you last eat?

3. **Click "Log Reading"**

4. **The system automatically calculates:**
   - **FBS (Fasting Blood Sugar):** If 8+ hours since last meal
   - **1 hr PP (Post-Prandial):** If 45-75 minutes after meal
   - **2 hr PP:** If 1.75-2.25 hours after meal
   - **RBS (Random Blood Sugar):** All other times

#### Glucose Guidelines

| Reading Type | Low | Normal | High |
|--------------|-----|--------|------|
| **Any Type** | < 70 | 70-130 | Varies |
| **Fasting (FBS)** | < 70 | 70-130 | > 130 |
| **After Meals** | < 70 | 70-180 | > 180 |

#### Important Alerts

**Low Glucose (< 70 mg/dL):**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **WARNING:** Low blood sugar
- **Consume a meal or fast-acting carbohydrate immediately**
- Examples: juice, glucose tablets, candy
- **Consider contacting emergency services if you feel unwell**

**High Glucose (> 130 FBS or > 180 other):**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **ALERT:** High blood sugar detected
- **An email is automatically sent to the office**
- Message confirms the alert was sent
- Office will follow up with you

## üìä Viewing Your Medical Records

### Medical Records Section

Located at the bottom of your dashboard, this section displays all your logged health readings.

### What You'll See

Each record shows:
- **Type:** Diabetes Log, Hypertension Log, or Weight Log
- **Subtype:** (For glucose) FBS, 1 hr PP, 2 hr PP, or RBS
- **Value:** Your actual reading
- **Unit:** mg/dL, mmHg, kg, etc.
- **Date & Time:** When the reading was taken
- **Flag:** Normal, High, or Low (if applicable)

### Color-Coded Alerts

- **Red Badge:** Critical reading (High or Low flag)
- **Normal readings:** No special badge

### Reading Details

**Glucose Readings:**
- Shows glucose value in mg/dL
- Displays reading type (FBS, RBS, etc.)

**Blood Pressure Readings:**
- Shows systolic/diastolic (e.g., 120/80 mmHg)
- Shows pulse (e.g., 70 bpm)
- Displays reading site

**Weight Readings:**
- Shows weight in kg

## üîî Understanding Alerts

### Email Alerts to Office

The system automatically sends email alerts to **office@aleracarecollective.com** for:

‚úÖ **High Glucose Readings**
- Fasting > 130 mg/dL
- Other readings > 180 mg/dL

‚úÖ **Hypertensive Crisis**
- Systolic > 180 mmHg
- OR Diastolic > 120 mmHg

‚úÖ **Low Blood Pressure**
- Systolic < 90 mmHg
- OR Diastolic < 60 mmHg

### What Happens After an Alert

1. **You see a dialog message** confirming the alert
2. **Email is sent to the office** automatically
3. **Office staff will review** your reading
4. **You may receive a follow-up call** from the office
5. **Consider contacting emergency services if you feel unwell**

### Critical Alerts

For dangerous readings:
- **DANGER dialogs** appear immediately
- **Email is sent to office**
- **You should consider seeking medical attention if you feel unwell**
- **Do not ignore these warnings**

## üåü Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is MyChart free to use?**
A: Yes, MyChart is provided free to all Alera Care Collective patients.

**Q: Can family members use my account?**
A: No, each person must have their own account for privacy and security.

**Q: Can I access MyChart on my phone?**
A: Yes! MyChart works on smartphones, tablets, and computers.

**Q: How long is my data stored?**
A: Your health records are stored indefinitely for your medical history.

### Logging Readings

**Q: What if I forget to log a reading?**
A: You can log it later by entering the correct date and time.

**Q: Can I edit a reading after logging it?**
A: Currently, you cannot edit readings. Contact the office if you made an error.

**Q: Do I need to log every reading I take?**
A: Log as recommended by your healthcare provider. More data helps with care.

**Q: What if my reading seems wrong?**
A: Take the reading again to confirm. If still unusual, contact your provider.

**Q: Will I get email notifications?**
A: Currently, you don't receive emails. The office receives alerts for critical readings.

**Q: What if I get a critical alert but feel fine?**
A: Take the reading again to confirm. If still critical, contact your provider as a precaution.

**Q: Why didn't my low glucose reading send an email?**
A: Low glucose alerts show a warning but don't send emails. Treat the low blood sugar immediately and consider contacting emergency services if you feel unwell.

### Privacy and Security

**Q: Who can see my health information?**
A: Only you and authorized Alera Care Collective staff members.

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes, we use industry-standard encryption and security measures.

## üìñ Glossary

**FBS (Fasting Blood Sugar):** Glucose reading taken 8+ hours after eating

**PP (Post-Prandial):** After eating. 1hr PP = 1 hour after meal, 2hr PP = 2 hours after meal

**RBS (Random Blood Sugar):** Glucose reading at any time

**Systolic:** The top number in blood pressure (pressure when heart beats)

**Diastolic:** The bottom number in blood pressure (pressure when heart rests)

**BPM:** Beats per minute (heart rate/pulse)

**mg/dL:** Milligrams per deciliter (unit for glucose)

**mmHg:** Millimeters of mercury (unit for blood pressure)

**Hypertensive Crisis:** Dangerously high blood pressure requiring immediate attention

**Comorbidities:** Other medical conditions you have

**Prescriptions:** Medications prescribed by your healthcare provider
